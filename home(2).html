BURNHAM PARK

Surrounded by pine trees, gardens, and scenic walking paths, Burhnham Park offers visitors a peaceful retreat while still being within walking distance of Baguio' City's busting commercial areas. It is often included in Baguio City tours, making it a must visit destination for those exploring the city.

MINES VIEW PARK

Nestled in the heart og Baguio City, Mines View park is a gem that promises a mix og breathtaking views, cultural immersion, and relaxing escape from the hustle anf bustle. Whether you're a seasoned traveler or a first time visitor, this iconic destination is sure to captivate you.

WRIGHT PARK

Mines View Park in Baguio offers a variety of activities including enjoying the scenic view, taking pony rides, visiting the pettting zoo, and purchasing souvenirs. You can also taste local delicaceis try on traditional Cordilerra regalia for photos and even ride horses.

THE MANSION

The Baguio Mansion House was originally built as the summer headquarters for American Governors-General. Eventually, it became the official summer residence of the President of the Philippines, starting during the Commonwealth period.

BOTANICAL GARDEN

Botanical Garden remains a popular destination for both tourists and residents. It is home to a vast collection of plant species, both indigenous and exotic, and has also become a space for showcasing the rich culture of the indigenous peoples of the Cordilleras.

CAMP JOHN HAY

Camp John Hay is a renowned mountain retreat in Baguio City. Built about 5000 feet above sea level, averaging a moderate temperature of 19 degree Celsius. In addition to hotels, shopping and conference centers in the city, it is visited mainly because of its world class Jack Nicklaus-designed golf course.

LA TRINIDAD STRAWBERRY FARM

The province's cool climate and highlands are conducive to harvesting farm produce and fruits like strawberries. La Trinidad is the capital of Benguet. It is also known as the Strawberry Capital of the Philippines due to its large plantation of luscious strawberries.

BAGUIO CATHEDRAL

Dedicated to the Blessed Virgin Mary under the title of Our Lady of Atonement, its distinctive exterior, twin spires and stained glass windows make it a popular tourist attraction in Baguio. It served as an evacuation center under the Japanese Occupation during the Second World War.

TAM-AWAN VILLAGE

More than just being a home for many artists and their masterpieces, the village is also labeled by some as a living museum as it has managed to salvage authentic huts from Sagada and Kalinga. This effort has since enabled visitors to have a glimpse of and experience Cordilleran culture.

BENCAP MUSEUM

Tourists looking to visit the Bencab Museum can expect a multi-sensory experience, with the museum's café and its farm-to-table concept offering an added delight to the gastronomically inclined traveler. The museum's location also allows for combining a cultural trip with the exploration of the surrounding Cordillera landscape.

SESSION ROAD

Session Road is Baguio's main thoroughfare in the Philippines. This street is the centre of the Baguio Central Business District. Previously, Session Road led to the old Baden Powell Hall. In this hall, the Philippine Commission held sessions from April 22 to June 11, 1904, and officially initiated the use of Baguio as the Summer Capital of the Philippines.

BAGUIO NIGHT MARKET

Baguio Night Market, commonly known as Harrison Road Night Market, is a must-visit shopping destination when in Baguio City, Philippines. Situated along Harrison Road, Baguio Night Market is ideal for those seeking affordable souvenirs and a taste of local culture. The market is bustling with various stalls offering Filipino handicrafts, local delicacies, clothing, shoes, and more.

PANAGBENGA FESTIVAL

Every year, the colorful and vibrant Panagbenga Festival blooms in Baguio, showcasing the region's rich floral beauty and cultural heritage. The Flower Festival started in 1995 to help Baguio City heal after a massive earthquake in 1990. "Panagbenga" means "season of blooming" in the Kankanaey dialect and symbolizes the city's strong community spirit. Now, it’s Baguio's biggest event, drawing large crowds every year. Explore the different ways to enjoy this vibrant celebration and why it remains a cherished event in the Philippines.

BAGUIO PUBLIC MARKET

Markets are one of the best ways to immerse yourself in local culture, sample exotic food, and get a sense of the traditional handicrafts or goods of that particular country or region. For those of you who are lucky enough to soon be traveling to the Philippines, allow me to give you some insight into my favorite market of the Philippines, the Baguio City Market.

MIRADOR HERITAGE AND ECO PARK

The park covers 9 hectares with different sections and attractions, a great choice whether you are alone in silence and prayer or spending quality time with family and friends. Walking through these new faces of Mirador invites you to revisit your inner self. From Igorot Stone Kingdom, we drove south to Mirador Heritage and Eco-Spirituality Park, situated on Mirador Hill. This place has been a pilgrimage site for some time. Now, the park features Japan-inspired sections that are great photo spots.

STRAWBERRY TAHO

The magic of strawberry taho in Baguio lies in the exclusive use of locally grown strawberries. Baguio's cool highland climate provides the optimal conditions for strawberry cultivation, resulting in an abundance of juicy, flavorful fruit.

STRAWBERRY JAM

Strawberry jam famous in Baguio, because Baguio is in high altitude, there is an abundance of fresh strawberries and strawberry products. The strawberries come from the farms around the area, especially its neighboring municipality, La Trinidad, which is dubbed as the Strawberry Capital of the Philippines

PINIKPIKAN

Pinikpikan is a chicken or duck dish from the mountains of the Cordillera region in the Philippines. As a tradition of the indigenous Igorot people, pinikpikan is prepared by beating a live chicken to death with a stick prior to cooking. The beating bruises the chicken's flesh by bringing blood to its surface, which is said to improve the flavour after cooking.

SUNDOT KULANGOT

Sundot Kulangot is a traditional candy made with coconut jam or kalamay, enclosed in small round containers or bao crafted from the seeds of a native tree called bitaog or bitaoy (Calophyllum inophyllum).

Itag

Smoked Meat Etag is the general term used in Cordillera about the native's traditional way of preserving pork. A slab of a big slice of pork is cured in salt for about a week (or longer) then air-dried under the sun or smoked for several weeks (even months), or both.

Tamales or Patupat

A traditional delicacy made from glutinous rice cooked in coconut milk and wrapped in woven coconut leaves. It's then boiled in sugarcane juice, giving it a distinct and delightful flavor. Perfect for those with a sweet tooth!

BINUNGOR

Binungor is one of the famous exotic delicacy of the province of Kalinga.Its main ingredients are agurong(stir fried water shells)mixed with rabbong (bamboo shoots) and the most important ingredients sichut (hot chili).

TAPUY

Tapuy, also spelled tapuey or tapey, is a rice wine produced in the Philippines. It is a traditional beverage and originated from Banaue and Mountain Province, where it is used for important occasions like weddings, rice harvesting ceremonies, fiestas and cultural fairs
